MARINE LIFE GALLERY

Shark

Sharks are a special type of fish known because their body is made out of cartilage instead of bones like other fish use their gills to filter oxygen from the water.

Sea horse

A seahorse is any of 46 species of small marine fish in the genus Hippocampus.Seahorses are unique in that the male seahorse is the one who carries the young and gives birth.

Alage

The scientific name of Red Algae is Rhodophyta and they belong to Class Rhodophyceae.Red algae are red because of the presence of the pigment phycoerythrin; this pigment reflects red light and absorbs blue light.

Turtle

Turtles are reptiles of the order Testudines, characterized by a special shell developed mainly from their ribs.Turtle shells are more streamlined to aid in swimming.

Rayfish

Batoidea is a superorder of cartilaginous fishes, commonly known as rays. They and their close relatives to shark.All are considered to be within a closely related group of fish called elasmobranchs

Sponges

Sponges — simple aquatic animals with dense, yet porous, skeletons — are highly adapted to their environments. A variety of sponges dot the seascape of Flower Garden Banks National Marine Sanctuary.

Starfish

starfish are invertebrates, they do have a kind of skeleton. The bodies of starfish are composed of calcium carbonate plates,they have bony, calcified skin, which protects them predators, and many wear colors.

Sargassum

Sargassum is a genus of brown macroalgae in the order Fucales of the Phaeophyceae class. Numerous species are distributed throughout the temperate and tropical oceans of the world

Jellyfish

A free-swimming marine coelenterate that is the sexually reproducing form of a hydrozoan or scyphozoan and has a nearly transparent saucer-shaped body

Sea cat

SeaCat was the marketing name used by Sea Containers Ferries Scotland for its services between Northern Ireland, Scotland and England between 1992 and 2004.

Sea lion

Sea lions are pinnipeds characterized by external ear flaps, long foreflippers, the ability to walk on all fours, short and thick hair, and a big chest and belly. Together with the fur seals.

Octupus

An octopus is a soft-bodied, eight-limbed mollusc of the order Octopoda. famous for their rounded bodies, bulging eyes.They live in all the world's oceans but are especially abundant in warm, tropical waters.

Dolphin

A dolphin is an aquatic mammal within the infraorder Cetacea. Dolphin species belong to the families Delphinidae,. There are 40 extant species named as dolphins.This is considered 'reflective' of their intelligence.

Squid

A squid is a mollusc with an elongated soft body, large eyes, eight arms, and two tentacles in the superorderSquid have been around for millions of years, and inspired legends of terrifying ship-devouring sea monsters

Whale

Whales are a widely distributed and diverse group of fully aquatic placental marine mammals. As an informal and colloquial grouping, they correspond to large members of the infraorder Cetacea

Coral

A coral reef is an underwater ecosystem characterized by reef-building corals. Reefs are formed of colonies of coral polyps held together by calcium carbonate

Angel fish

Pterophyllum is a small genus of freshwater fish from the family Cichlidae known to most aquarists as angelfish. All Pterophyllum species originate from the Amazon Basin

clownfish

The ocellaris clownfish, also known as the false percula clownfish or common clownfish, is a marine fish belonging to the family Pomacentridae, which includes clownfishes and damselfishes

Eel

Eels are ray-finned fish belonging to the order Anguilliformes, which consists of eight suborders, 20 families, 164 genera, and about 1000 species.Eels undergo considerable development from the early larval stage

Crab

Crabs are decapod crustaceans of the infraorder Brachyura, which typically have a very short projecting "tail", usually hidden entirely under the thorax. They live in all the world's oceans, in freshwater
