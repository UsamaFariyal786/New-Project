Welcome To Jenkinson Sea Life

Marine life, sea life, or ocean life is the plants, animals, and other organisms that live in the salt water of seas or oceans, or the brackish water of coastal estuaries. At a fundamental level, marine life affects the nature of the planet. Marine organisms, mostly microorganisms, produce oxygen and sequester carbon.

Goldfish are a type of domesticated carp that originated in China over 1,000 years ago. They are popular as pets and come in many varieties of colors, shapes, and sizes. Dolphin

Dolphins are aquatic mammals that belong to the infraorder Cetacea. There are 36 dolphin species, found in every ocean and some rivers. Dolphins have a streamlined body, a long snout with teeth, flippers, and a dorsal fin. They use echolocation to find prey and communicate with each other. Dolphins are social animals that live in pods and have complex vocalizations. Dolphins are also intelligent and can learn tricks, use tools, and recognize themselves in mirrors
